Origins

The golden retriever breed was originally bred in Scotland in the mid-19th century. They were used to retrieve prey such as ducks and other game birds. Known for their soft mouths, the Golden Retriever is able to carry objects without damaging them, making them ideal for retrieving. Golden Retrievers are also prone to be swimmers, as their outer coat repels water by lying flat against their body. There are primarily three types of Golden Retrievers: English, Canadian, and American, with golden, light golden, and dark golden coats. Canadian goldens tend to have a thinner coat than American goldens, while English goldens tend to have lighter fur colors. Dudley Marjoribanks has also been credied for developing the Golden Retriver Breed by breeding a Tweed water spaniel with a yellow retriever. The puppies were then breed with other dogs, such as black retrievers, St. John's water dog, and the Irish setter.
