Origins

The golden retriever breed was originally bred in Scotland in the mid-19th century. They were used to retrieve prey such as ducks and other game birds. Known for their soft mouths, the Golden Retriever is able to carry objects without damaging them, making them ideal for retrieving. Golden Retrievers are also prone to be swimmers, as their outer coat repels water by lying flat against their body. Dudley Marjoribanks has also been credited for developing the Golden Retriver Breed by breeding a Tweed water spaniel with a yellow retriever. The puppies were then breed with other dogs, such as black retrievers, St. John's water dog, and the Irish setter. As the dog became more popular in England, the Golden Retriever would eventually be recognized by The Kennel Club of England in 1911. For more information, please use this link!

American retrievers

There are primarily three types of golden retrievers: English, Canadian, and American, with varying golden, light golden, and dark golden coats. American golden retrievers are typically smaller, with less muscular bodies. They also tend to have darker coat colors and triangular-shaped eyes.

English retrievers

English golden retrievers typically have lighter coat colors. They have a broader head and have rounder, darker eyes than the American golden retrievers. These golden retrievers also tend to be more muscular than American golden retrievers.

Canadian retrievers

Canadian golden retrievers usually have thinner, shorter coats. Their fur is also denser, and are typically taller than their English and American counterparts.
